Ming Underground Mine

The Ming Mine is a historic volcanogenic massive sulphide (VMS) copper-gold deposit located in the Baie Verte Mining District. FireFly Metals has significantly expanded the known mineralisation through systematic drilling, with the deposit now extending over 1.2 km of strike length and remaining open at depth. The mine hosts high-grade copper, gold, zinc, and silver mineralisation across multiple lenses.

Additional Deposits

Beyond the Ming Mine, the Green Bay project encompasses a portfolio of satellite deposits including Ming South, Rambler, and multiple untested VMS targets. These deposits offer substantial resource growth potential within a proven geological setting, with historical drilling returning significant intercepts of copper-gold mineralisation across the broader property package.

Jurisdiction

Newfoundland and Labrador ranks among the world's premier mining jurisdictions. Ranked #6 globally for policy perception and #8 for investment attractiveness by the Fraser Institute, the province offers a supportive regulatory framework, skilled workforce, and established mining infrastructure that de-risks project development.

Infrastructure

The project benefits from exceptional existing infrastructure including the Pine Cove Port facility, providing direct tidewater access for concentrate shipping. Grid power, sealed roads, and nearby communities with experienced mining workforces significantly reduce capital requirements and development timelines compared to remote greenfield projects.
