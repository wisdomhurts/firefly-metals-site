Historic Mine

The Pickle Crow Mine historically produced over 1.5 million ounces of gold at an exceptional average grade of approximately 16 g/t Au between 1935 and 1966. The mine operated across multiple high-grade vein systems within a prolific Archean greenstone belt, establishing a proven track record of significant gold endowment in the district. Modern exploration has confirmed that substantial mineralisation remains beyond the historic workings.

Exploration Upside

Significant exploration potential remains across the broader Pickle Crow property, with multiple high-grade vein systems extending beyond the historic mine workings. Recent drilling has intersected new zones of gold mineralisation at depth and along strike, demonstrating that the known resource represents only a fraction of the total gold endowment. The project offers exceptional resource growth potential through systematic modern exploration techniques.

Why Pickle Crow?

At 7.2 g/t gold, Pickle Crow ranks among the highest-grade undeveloped gold resources globally. For context, the global average grade for gold mines in production is approximately 1.5 g/t — making Pickle Crow nearly five times the global average. This exceptional grade translates to significantly higher margins and lower per-ounce operating costs compared to industry peers.

Located in Ontario, Canada — the world's #1 ranked mining jurisdiction by the Fraser Institute for investment attractiveness — Pickle Crow benefits from established infrastructure, a supportive permitting environment, and proximity to experienced mining communities. Ontario's track record of responsible resource development provides a stable foundation for advancing this world-class asset.
